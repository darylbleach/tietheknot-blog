## What to look for in a wedding website builder

Before comparing platforms, it helps to know what actually matters. The things you'll care about on the day are:

**RSVPs that work.** Chasing guests for replies via WhatsApp is miserable. A good wedding website collects RSVPs digitally, with dietary requirements and plus-ones built in.

**Guest information in one place.** Venue address, travel directions, accommodation options, parking, dress code, ceremony time — all of it, accessible from one link.

**A design you're proud to send.** Your wedding website is often the first impression guests get of your big day. A template that looks beautiful matters.

**No nasty surprises.** Some platforms are free upfront but lock key features behind paywalls, show ads on your page, or start charging monthly fees after a trial period.

### Free wedding website platforms

**Bridebook** and **Hitched** are the two biggest free options built specifically for the UK market. Both offer decent RSVP tools, are integrated with UK gift registries, and have been around long enough to be trusted.

The trade-off: free platforms are ad-supported, have limited design options, and don't give you full ownership of your site. Some features — like removing branding or unlocking premium templates — cost extra. There can also be data privacy implications worth knowing about.

**Zola** and **The Knot** are popular in the US and work for UK couples, but their defaults (currency, registry partners, date formats) are American. Fine if you don't mind adjusting, but not built with you in mind.
