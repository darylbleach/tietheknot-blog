# How Much Does a Wedding Website Cost in the UK?

You've just got engaged. The to-do list is growing by the hour, and someone's already asked whether you have a wedding website yet. Before you sign up for the first free platform you find, it's worth understanding what you're actually paying for — and what "free" really means.

Wedding website costs in the UK range from £0 to several hundred pounds, depending on the platform and what features you need. Here's a clear-eyed look at each option.

## Free Wedding Websites: What You're Actually Paying With

Several platforms offer free wedding websites, and they're genuinely usable. Bridebook and Hitched are the two biggest UK options, and both let you build a basic site without spending a penny.

The catch? Free platforms are typically ad-supported. Your guests visit a page you've lovingly put together — and they might see banner adverts for competing venues, registrars, or wedding suppliers. The experience isn't quite the polished, personal touch most couples are hoping for.

Free tiers also tend to come with design limitations. You'll often be restricted to a handful of templates, limited colour options, and branding you can't fully remove. If you want your wedding website to feel like *your* wedding, that can be frustrating.

That said, if budget is genuinely tight, a free platform is far better than no website at all.

## Subscription-Based Platforms

US platforms like The Knot and Zola offer subscription tiers, typically starting around $10–20 per month (roughly £8–16). They're polished and feature-rich, but built primarily for American couples. Guest addresses, gift registries, and vendor directories are all oriented towards the US market, which can feel awkward if you're planning a wedding in Birmingham or Edinburgh.

Some UK couples do use them successfully, but you may find yourself working around features that don't quite fit — or paying monthly for a platform that's only 90% relevant to your situation.

## One-Time Payment Platforms

A growing number of wedding website builders charge a single flat fee with no ongoing subscription. This model makes a lot of sense for weddings: you need the site for roughly 12–18 months, not forever, so paying month-by-month ends up more expensive than it first appears.

## What Features Should You Expect to Pay For?

Not all wedding websites are equal. Before choosing a platform, check what's included — particularly around RSVP management.

### RSVP Management

Basic RSVP tools let guests mark themselves as attending or not. Better platforms let you collect dietary requirements, song requests, plus-one confirmations, and accommodation preferences all in one place. That data then lives in a tidy dashboard rather than scattered across email inboxes.

### Gift List Integration

Some platforms let you link directly to a gift list or registry. This saves guests from hunting through separate tabs and keeps everything in one convenient place.

### Design and Templates

The number of templates varies enormously. Three well-designed, thoughtfully built templates will often serve you better than thirty mediocre ones. What matters is whether the final result looks like something you'd be proud to share.

### Photo Galleries and Event Timelines

A good wedding website does more than confirm the venue and date. Galleries let you share engagement photos; timeline sections walk guests through the day; travel and accommodation pages save you answering the same questions a hundred times over.

## Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

With free and subscription platforms, watch for:

- **Domain customisation fees** — some platforms charge extra to use your own domain (e.g., sarah-and-james.co.uk)
- **Premium template fees** — the nicest designs are sometimes locked behind an upgrade
- **RSVP limits** — a few platforms cap the number of RSVP responses on free or lower tiers
- **Removal of platform branding** — getting rid of "Powered by [Platform]" often costs extra

With a one-time platform, what you see is what you get. No surprises mid-planning.
