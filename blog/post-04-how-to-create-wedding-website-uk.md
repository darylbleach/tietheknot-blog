## Step 2: Choose your design

Most wedding website platforms offer a handful of templates. Don't overthink this. Pick something that feels like you and matches the general vibe of your wedding.

If your wedding is modern and minimal, go with something clean. If it's romantic and classic, pick something with elegant typography and warm colours. If it's traditional and formal, go with something timeless.

## Step 3: Add the core content

Work through this in order — it's the fastest way to get a complete website live:

**1. Your names and wedding date.**
First thing on the page, unmissable.

**2. Ceremony and reception details.**
Full venue names and addresses, arrival time, ceremony start time, and what happens when (ceremony → drinks → wedding breakfast → evening reception).

**3. RSVP.**
Set up your RSVP form, choose your deadline, and decide what information you need from guests (dietary requirements, song requests, plus-ones).

**4. Travel information.**
Full postcode for sat-nav, parking details, nearest train station, taxi options.

**5. Accommodation.**
Any hotel block bookings you've arranged, plus a few nearby alternatives for guests who need to book their own.

**6. Gift list.**
If you have one, add the link. If you haven't set up your gift list yet, come back to this later.

## Step 4: Add the extras

![Couple sharing their wedding website link with guests via smartphone](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1529488978989-e75b83f8a8d7?w=1200&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

Once the essentials are done, these make your website feel personal rather than functional:

**Your story.** A short "how we met / how we got engaged" section. Guests love it, and it takes 20 minutes to write.

**Photos.** A few photos of you both — engagement shots, holiday snaps, anything that represents you. You don't need a professional photoshoot for this.

**FAQ.** Go through your wedding planning group chats and emails, find every question you've been asked, and answer them here. Parking? Kids? Dress code? Evening only? This section saves you weeks of individual replies.

---

## Step 5: Password-protect it

Before you share the link, add a password. Your wedding website contains your venue address, your schedule, and personal details — you don't want it publicly indexed on Google where anyone can find it.

Choose something simple that guests will remember (or include in the same message as the URL). Avoid anything too obscure.

---

## Step 6: Share it with guests

The best time to share your wedding website is with your save-the-dates — so guests have the link from the very start. Include it on the physical card if you're sending them, and share it in a personal message too.

In your message, tell guests the password (if you've set one), remind them to RSVP by a specific date, and let them know they can find everything they need on the website.

---

## How long does it take?

Most couples spend 2–4 hours building their wedding website from scratch. If you've got your venue details confirmed and a few photos ready, it can be quicker.

The RSVP setup and FAQ sections tend to take the longest. Everything else — names, dates, venue — is usually done in the first 30 minutes.
